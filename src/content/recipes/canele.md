## Heat the milk

In a saucepan, combine **500 ml (2 cups + 2 tbsp) cold milk** and **1 tsp vanilla bean powder**. Bring to a gentle simmer, then remove from heat.

![Milk and vanilla in saucepan](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7973.jpg)

![Milk coming to a simmer](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7974.jpg)

## Mix the sugar and eggs

In a large bowl, whisk together **250 g (1 cup) sugar**, **2 large eggs**, and **2 egg yolks** until combined. Stir in **50 g (3½ tbsp) melted butter**.

![Sugar, eggs, and yolks](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7975.jpg)

![Whisking together](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7976.jpg)

![Adding melted butter](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7977.jpg)

![Egg mixture combined](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7978.jpg)

## Temper the eggs

Add about ¼ of the hot milk mixture to the egg mixture, stirring constantly to temper the eggs and prevent them from scrambling.

<figure>
  <img src="/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7984.jpg" alt="Tempering the eggs with hot milk" />
  <figcaption>You can see some small clumps of flour in my batter here. I was worried at the time but these ended up smoothing out.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Add the flour

Add **100 g (½ cup) flour** and stir until smooth and consistent.

![Adding flour to the batter](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7983.jpg)

## Add the remaining milk

Pour in the remaining hot milk mixture, stirring constantly until fully incorporated.

## Add rum

Stir in **60 ml (1 shot) dark rum**.

![Adding dark rum](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7985.jpg)

## Rest

Cover the batter and refrigerate for at least 24 hours, and up to 4 days. This rest is essential — don't skip it.

![Batter covered and ready to rest](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7995.jpg)

## Coat the molds

Preheat your oven to **550°F (290°C)**.

Melt together **40 g beeswax** and **60 g butter** (a 2:3 ratio). Pour or brush the mixture into your canelé molds, coating them evenly.

![Mold coating ingredients](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7990.jpg)

![Melting the beeswax and butter](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7991.jpg)

![Coating the molds](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7993.jpg)

![Molds coated and ready](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7994.jpg)

![Brushing the molds](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7997.jpg)

![Coated molds close-up](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_8006.jpg)

## Fill the molds

Whisk the batter to bring it back together, then pour into the prepared molds, leaving about **1 cm of space at the top**.

</video>

![Filled molds](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7998.jpg)

![Molds ready to bake](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_7999.jpg)

## Bake

Bake at **550°F for 10 minutes**, then — without opening the oven — reduce the temperature to **375°F (190°C)** and bake for another **45–50 minutes**, until deeply caramelized.

<video autoplay loop muted playsinline class="w-full rounded-xl my-6">
  <source src="/images/recipes/canele/IMG_8001_trim.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>

## Unmold + cool

Remove from the oven and immediately unmold onto a cooling rack. They should release easily.

![Unmolding the canelés](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_8002.jpg)

<video autoplay loop muted playsinline class="w-full rounded-xl my-6">
  <source src="/images/recipes/canele/IMG_8003.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>

![Finished canelés](/images/recipes/canele/IMG_8004.jpg)

Wait at least **2 hours** before serving — the interior needs time to set.
